The Nightingale

Author: Kristin Hannah

About author: Kristin Hannah is an American author known for her bestselling novels that explore the complexities of family relationships and the endurance of the human spirit. She has written over 20 books, including The Nightingale, which has sold over 4 million copies worldwide.

Category: Historical Fiction

The Nightingale

Vianne Mauriac says goodbye to her husband, Antoine, as he leaves the quiet town of Carriveau to go to the Front. She doesn't think the Nazis will come into France...But they do attack, with hordes of soldiers moving, convoys of trucks and tanks, and planes that fill the sky and drop bombs on the innocent. When a German captain takes over Vianne's house, she and her daughter have to live with the enemy or lose everything. Without food, money, or hope, and with danger growing all around them, she has to make impossible choices after impossible choices to keep her family alive. Isabelle, Vianne's sister, is an 18-year-old troublemaker who is looking for a cause with all the wildfire of youth. While thousands of Parisians march into the unknown dangers of war, she meets Gatan, a rebel who thinks the French can fight the Nazis from within France, and she falls in love as only young people can: totally. But when he betrays her, Isabelle joins the Resistance and never looks back, putting her life in danger over and over again to save others.
